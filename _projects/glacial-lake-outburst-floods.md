A fourth thread confronts the hazards posed by a rapidly changing cryosphere. As glaciers retreat, meltwater collects in ice-marginal and moraine-dammed lakes that can fail catastrophically, releasing glacial-lake outburst floods (GLOFs) that travel far downstream. We have mapped how these lakes are evolving across Alaska and High Mountain Asia, characterized how their outburst behavior is changing, and worked to build science-based systems for forecasting GLOF hazard. {% cite jacquet_hydrologic_2017 rick_dam_2022 rick_unchanged_2023 %}

Recent work tracks the rapid growth of ice-marginal lakes driven by glacier retreat, and the seasonal-to-decadal dynamics of lakes perched on debris-covered glaciers. These observations anchor the forecasts communities will need as the cryosphere continues to change. {% cite mcgrath_rapid_2026 zeller_seasonal_2024 %}
